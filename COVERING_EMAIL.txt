Hi colleagues,

Over the past months, I've been developing a customizable rubric framework for assessing assignments where students engage with AI tools. This work has been shaped by our teaching in the School of Management and Marketing and tested with real assignments (like our CloudCore audit). We're now releasing this resource to the wider school community.

I'm attaching four Word documents that form a complete suite. Here's how to use them:

**THE WORKFLOW (3 STEPS)**

1. Start with: 1_README_Getting_Started_v2.docx
   → Quick overview of the philosophy and what you'll find

2. Review: 2_Generic_Rubric_Template_v2.docx
   → This is your core framework (5 criteria, 4-level scoring)
   → Works across disciplines and AI tools

3. Customise: Use 3_Adaptation_Guide_v2.docx
   → Shows exactly how to tailor the rubric for your context
   → Includes examples for LLMs, chatbots, code assistants, data tools, creative AI

4. (Optional) See: 4_Example_CloudCore_Audit_v2.docx
   → Concrete example from an information security audit
   → Shows what a customised rubric looks like in practice


**WHY THIS MATTERS**

These rubrics are designed around one key principle: critical thinking about AI tools matters more than speed of tool use. Students should learn to evaluate AI outputs, understand limitations, and take responsibility for final work—not just accept AI suggestions uncritically.

If that aligns with what you're trying to teach, this framework is for you.


**WHAT TO DO NEXT**

Pick a unit where you're thinking about integrating AI assessment:
- Download the adapted rubric template
- Customise it for your specific context (the guide tells you how)
- Use it with your students

If you get stuck or have questions, happy to workshop this with you.
